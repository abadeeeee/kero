About Me

I'm Kerolos, a poet, educator, and the proud author of Indefinable, a collection of poems in Egyptian colloquial Arabic. As an English Language Arts and ESL teacher, I see education not just as instruction—but as inspiration. I believe that every learner holds the potential to become a living poem—full of meaning, emotion, and power to influence the world.

Fueled by a deep passion for language, I dedicate myself to reshaping mindsets, cultivating critical thinking, and nurturing students into global citizens who act with empathy and purpose. Through dynamic teaching, literature, cultural exchange, and creative expression, I strive to build classrooms that are inclusive, expressive, and transformative.

With TESOL certification and a strong foundation in American, British, and National Curricula, I bring years of experience in curriculum design, educational leadership, differentiated instruction, and student empowerment. I've authored English improvement booklets and standardized test prep resources, and I lead initiatives that blend academic achievement with emotional intelligence and civic responsibility.

Whether I'm mentoring students to present before university professors, organizing community service visits to orphanages, or simply celebrating the power of words in a quiet classroom moment—I teach to inspire change.
